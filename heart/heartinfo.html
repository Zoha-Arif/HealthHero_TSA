The Heart

The heart is a muscle in your body. The heart's job is to pump blood throughout your body. The heart is part of the circulatory system.
























The heart is made up of four chambers: right atrium, right ventricle, left atrium, and left ventricle. The right atrium receives blood from the veins and pumps it to the right ventricle. The right ventricle receives blood from the right atrium and pumps it to the lungs, where it is loaded with oxygen. The left atrium receives oxygenated blood from the lungs and pumps it to the left ventricle. The left ventricle pumps oxygen-rich blood to the rest of the body.

The heart has valves. The mitral and tricuspid valves control blood flow from the atria to the ventricles. The aortic and pulmonary valves control blood flow out of the ventricles.

The heart has a heart wall. The heart wall is made up of three layers: epicardium, myocardium, and endocardium. The epicardium is the outer layer of the heart wall. The myocardium is the thick, middle layer of the heart wall. The myocardium contains cardiac muscle tissue. The endocardium is the inner layer of the heart wall.

Facts About the Heart

  • The heart beats about 115,000 times a day.
  • The heart pumps about 2,000 gallons of blood a day.
  • If you were to stretch out your blood vessel system, it would extend over 60,000 miles.
  • The beating sound of a heart is caused by the valves of the heart opening and closing.
  • The heart can continue beating even when it is disconnected from the body.

Heart Disease

Types of Heart Disease

A few examples of types of heart disease include:

  • Arteriosclerosis / Atherosclerosis
  • Endothelial Dysfunction
  • Coronary Artery Disease
  • Stroke
  • Heart Failure
  • Heart Arrhythmias
  • Valve Disorders
  • Peripheral Artery Disease
  • Cognitive Decline

Tips for Keeping Your Heart Healthy

Don't Smoke

Smoking is a major risk factor that may lead to heart disease, since chemicals in tobacco can damage your heart and your blood vessels. When you smoke, your arteries become narrow because of plaque buildup. Smoking can cause atherosclerosis and heart attack. Generally, using tobacco is harmful for your health, so you should avoid using it.

Exercise Regularly

Exercising regularly is important for maintaining your heart health. Physical activity helps to reduced your chances of developing conditions which increase your risk of heart disease, like high blood pressure, high cholestorol, and diabetes.

Eat Healthy Foods

Exercising healthy foods is important for maintaining your heart health. You should eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and whole grains. You should limit the amount of salt, sugar, saturated fat, and trans fat in your diet. A few examples of foods that are good for you are beans, low-fat or fat-free dairy products, lean meats, and fish.

Maintain a Healthy Weight

It is important to maintain a healthy weight, since being overweight increases your risk of heart disease. Also, being overweight increases chances of developing conditions like high blood pressure, high cholestorol, and diabetes.

Get Enough Quality Sleep

Sleep deprivation can harm your health, so it is important that you get enough quality sleep every night. Sleep deprivation increases your risk of obesity, high blood pressure, heart attack, diabetes, and depression.

Manage Stress

Managing stress is important for maintaining your heart health. A few examples of unhealthy ways to manage stress are overeating, drinking, and smoking. A few examples of healthy ways to manage stress are physical activity, relxation exercises, and meditation.

Get Regular Health Screenings

It is important to get regular health screenings to make sure that your heart is healthy.
